# 📝 Project Summary

This project features an end-to-end data analysis of the winter fashion market. Using a multi-year dataset, I developed a Tableau dashboard to identify shifts in consumer behavior, pricing strategies across major brands, and material preferences. The dashboard serves as a strategic tool for retail inventory planning and trend forecasting.

# 🎯 Business Objectives

Price Optimization: Identify the price ceiling for high-demand categories like Outerwear and Hoodies.

Material Strategy: Analyze which materials (Fleece vs. Wool vs. Cashmere) correlate with higher popularity scores.

Seasonality Tracking: Compare brand performance across three consecutive winter seasons to identify growth leaders.

Demographic Targeting: Understand the distribution of styles across Men, Women, and Unisex segments.

# 🛠️ Technical Stack & Skills

BI Tool: Tableau Desktop / Tableau Public

Data Cleaning: Excel 

Key Tableau Techniques:

Calculated Fields: Derived Year-over-Year (YoY) growth and average popularity metrics.

Level of Detail (LOD) Expressions: Used for fixed average pricing across categories regardless of filters.

Advanced Formatting: Implemented Lollipop charts and custom Heatmaps for better data density.

Dynamic Dashboarding: Integrated global parameters for Brand and Category filtering.

# 📑 Key Data Insights
Premium Categories: Hoodies ($507.6) and Coats ($506.0) represent the highest average price points, indicating a "premium" consumer segment.

The "Fleece" Factor: Despite Wool being a traditional winter staple, Fleece (159.50) and Cotton (154.00) emerged as the most popular materials by score.

Brand Growth: Analysis shows Mango significantly scaled its winter presence, growing its seasonal score from 19.90 in 2023 to 42.20 in 2025.

Market Gap: Accessories like Gloves have the lowest average price ($381.2), suggesting an entry-level market opportunity for luxury branding.
